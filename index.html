What Goes In

Natural
Ingredients

Every bottle begins in the field, not the lab. We work with coconut, rosemary, aloe, and wild herbs sourced from farms we visit ourselves — cold-pressed at low temperatures to keep their nutrients whole. No synthetic fragrance, no mineral oil, no fillers — just what the plant already gives.

Coconut forms our base for a reason: its fatty acids penetrate the hair shaft rather than sitting on top, so strands stay moisturized without weighing them down. Rosemary and our wild herbal blend are steeped slowly to draw out the compounds known to support circulation at the scalp, while cold-pressed aloe adds a light, soothing layer that calms irritation and flaking.

We test every harvest for purity before it goes anywhere near a bottle, and we never dilute a batch to stretch it further. It's a slower, more expensive way to make hair oil — but it's the only way we're willing to make it.
